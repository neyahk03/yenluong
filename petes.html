Role: UI/UX designer

My team: Yi Huan Yang, Sreeja Satish and Emma Houghton

Tool and Technologies: Figma, User Research, Affinity Mapping, NUF Framework, User Journey Map

Project Overview

This project is part of the User Experience course taught by Professor Chris Nappi. It involved designing a take-out app for Pete's Little Lunch Box, a popular food truck near Drexel University. Known for its quick, customizable meals but lacking branding, Pete's was an ideal candidate for a digital makeover. Through collaborative efforts, our team utilized user research, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing to create an app that reflected Pete's charm and practicality while addressing user needs and aligning with its values.

Challenge

We chose Pete's Little Lunch Box for its strong community presence and charm, aiming to translate this into a mobile app. Despite a small menu, their customizable options created a deceptively long list, challenging us to simplify it without overwhelming users. Additionally, balancing our team's workload and schedules proved difficult, but through communication and efficient use of class time, we collaborated effectively to deliver a comprehensive app.

Our Goal

Design an efficient and intuitive app

Reflect Pete's consistent reliability, friendly service, and commitment to quality

Cater to the fast-paced needs of on-the-go consumers

Empathize

To kickstart the project, we initiated a Fly on the Wall exercise, where each team member observed customers interacting with the food truck at different times of the day. In addition, we conducted interviews with Pete's customers to gain deeper insights into their needs and pain points.

Key Finding:
  • Regular customers tend to know their orders beforehand and spend little time on the menu
  • Non-regular customers take longer to decide and engage with the menu more
  • While waiting, customers typically engage with phones, chat with companions, or stand quietly
  • Payments are split between Venmo and cash, with Venmo being slightly more popula
  • Busy periods align with mealtimes, particularly lunch, with a rush occurring after class hours

During our User Research Interviews, we also conducted competitive research on existing food truck apps in the market. Additionally, we took an in-depth look at the mobile food ordering process to find inspiration and examples of intuitive and streamlined workflows. Define Phase

After getting enough insights from the customers and competitors, we define 4 user personas which represent each type of the customer and their journey.

Our primary users included students from Drexel and Penn, along with local professors, faculty, and workers seeking a quick and convenient meal. These groups were identified as the most likely to consistently use the app. Additionally, we considered tourists and visiting families as part of our customer base, though their usage of the app would likely be less frequent.

Form there, we developed a visual representation of the user's journey across all the touchpoints of our application to understand where we can improve the user experience.

Ideate Phase

In this phase, we conducted card sorting and performed a NUF (New, Useful, and Feasible) analysis, evaluating features on a 7-point scale to prioritize and refine our design decisions.

NUF Analysis

After brainstorming and identifying all the potential features for our app, we moved on to sketching and user flow. Each team member contributed unique ideas, and we selected the best elements from each concept to create a cohesive wireframe and paper prototype.

For the moodboard, each team member created a unique version, and we combined elements from all of them to design a unified moodboard. Our goal was to evoke a comforting feel through the color palette while retaining the red from Pete's original branding. We also incorporated a lunchbox icon and other signature elements to stay true to Pete's established brand identity.

Test Phase

Below is the evolution of our prototype, showcasing multiple iterations that incorporate feedback gathered from users during several rounds of testing. Each iteration reflects improvements made to enhance usability, address pain points, and align the design more closely with user needs.

Home Screen

Problem:
  • The carousel lacked functionality and moved automatically, which confused users.
  • Icons on the navigation bar were unclear and difficult to interpret. For instance, the menu button resembled a file cabinet in the initial two designs.
  • Critical information, such as the food truck's hours and location, was hidden under a dropdown menu, making it less accessible to users.
Solution
  • Users can now control the carousel at their own pace, and clicking on it directs them to the menu screen.
  • Updated the menu icon to make it more recognizable, added labels to all icons for better clarity and accessibility.
  • Displayed critical information, such as the food truck's hours and location, prominently on the home screen for quick access

Customization Screen

As mentioned above, although Pete's Little Lunch Box has a relatively small menu, it offers extensive customization options for each item. To accommodate this, our team decided to incorporate a customizable system within the app to streamline the menu and provide users with a more intuitive ordering experience.

Problem:
  • There were too many buttons on the screen, which could confuse users during the ordering process.
  • The radio buttons used to indicate selected items were helpful but too small and hard to see, especially when overlaid on images. To address this, we replaced them with a background color change for selected options.
Solution:
  • We prioritized reducing screen clutter by removing the navigation bar during the ordering process, helping users focus on their selections.
  • While the background color change improved visibility and usability, our professor pointed out the potential accessibility issues for users with disabilities. To address this, we reintroduced the radio button option while retaining the background color change, ensuring both accessibility and clarity.

Reward System

Problem:
  • The existing loyalty program's point system design was ambiguous, leading to user confusion and frustration.
  • Users mentioned the point system resembled a switch or reminded them of "Burger King," which caused confusion about its purpose.
  • The overall reward system lacked clarity. Users struggled to understand how points were earned, accumulated, and ultimately redeemed for rewards. This lack of transparency led to frustration and disengagement with the program.
Solution:
  • A comprehensive redesign of the reward system was implemented to enhance user understanding and engagement. This included clear and concise explanations of how points are earned, tracked, and redeemed.
  • We also relocated the location feature to the home screen to enhance visibility.

Takeaways

This project was a formative experience, combining our ideas into functional prototypes. Our redesigned app bridges the gap for Pete's Little Lunch Box by offering a streamlined menu and ordering system, making it more accessible to a larger community. Given more time, we'd enhance the menu and checkout flows for better user exploration and add a settings feature for light/dark mode. We also plan to include an option to contact the food truck for customer concerns.

Collaboration this term was a journey. Despite scheduling challenges due to extracurricular commitments, we effectively communicated via Discord to complete the project. Working through these difficulties allowed us to deliver a great final product.

The project was an invaluable opportunity to engage in an end-to-end design process. Through multiple iterations and user testing, we learned to incorporate feedback and continuously improve. Group critiques were also essential in refining the app, ensuring we received diverse insights.

Ultimately, this project deepened our understanding of UX principles. Using tools like Figma, FigJam, and Figma Slides, we applied key concepts from lectures, such as avoiding overwhelming users with too many navigation options. This focus improved usability, making our app smoother and easier to navigate.
